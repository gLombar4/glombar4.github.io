What is SMT?

Shin Megami Tensei (SMT) is a Japanese Role Playing Game series developed by Atlus. The main series gameplay is turn-based, though there are many spin-off titles that change up this formula. This is my personal fan site to show my appreciation for the series, and hopefully give you, the reader, some cool info on it! As of now, the most recent game in the series is the re-release of SMT V (SMT V: Vengeance), released in 2024.

My Personal Experience With the Series

Any discussion of SMT today is impossible without mention of Persona, and this page will be no exception. Persona began as a spinoff of SMT, but has since blossomed into something more successful than its progenitor. The very thing that got me started in this series was playing Persona 5 back in high school. Despite its widespread acclaim as a game series now, SMT has its roots in the "Digital Devil Story" novel series authored by Aya Nishitani (The first item in the table above).

Many games adapting this first novel have been made, but Atlus has since taken the series in their own direction starting with "Digital Devil Story: Megami Tensei II" for the Super Famicom in 1990.

As you can probably tell from the table above, there has been a wide gap in main series releases since Nocturne. Spin-offs, the sister series Persona, and upgraded rereleases keep the fans happy in the off periods.

SMT on Different Platforms

SMT has seen entries on many, many platforms. Even ones you may not expect like iOS and flip phones. Today its primarily found on all the major platforms you're familiar with like PS5, Switch, XBOX and PC. Unfortunately, some titles are either landlocked to their console (SMT IV on the 3DS), or untranslated into english. Emulation is sometimes the only viable way to play the older series titles.

SMT IV, for instance, is available on no platforms except the 3DS. A console whose e-shop service has been taken online, and is now left to the whims of scalpers on the internet. Emulation really is more convenient, which is a shame.

Gameplay Basics and How to Get Started

Depending on what title you're playing the rules may vary, but the heart of SMT's gameplay is the press turn combat system. Here's how it works: you are given a set amount of turns when you enter combat. Hitting an enemy weakness will add half a turn to your side. Having your attack nullified by an enemy will delete 2 turns from your side. There's some more intricacies but that's the gist. Where things get interesting is that these rules also apply to the enemy. What this means is that you're carefully considering what your party is weak to and what weaknesses they can target.

The other core system of SMT is demon fusion. You'll have to create a party while keeping in mind what weaknesses they have and what they can target. You recruit demons out in battle & then fuse them together to create stronger ones, keeping useful skills along the way. All of this combines into a system that will keep you engaged for the entirety of a game's runtime.

The Persona series uses a similar system called "One More" / "Baton Pass". It's the same loop of striking and protecting weaknesses, but a little more simplified. I reccommend starting with Persona if the series interests you!

Why Play This Series?

If you are drawn to turn-based games then I've no doubt you'll find SMT unforgettable. If you're unsure where to start, try Persona. That's where I started after all! The music, atmosphere, and themes tying the series together are quite compelling, and unlike anything I've seen in another game before or since. Not to mention the gameplay is just fun by itself.
